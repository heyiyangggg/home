Urban development, energy and climate justice, AI and labour — these aren't separate fields to me. Scroll to see where my projects sit at their intersections.

Urban & Grassroots

How cities are
made — and
by whom.

Urban development is never just technocratic planning. It is shaped by political institutions, grassroots movements, and the social histories embedded in place. I'm drawn to how these forces interact — from Singapore's planned towns to caste and social inequality in global cities.

Urban × Energy — Climate Justice

Who pays for
the energy
transition?

Urban space and energy systems are deeply entangled. Climate transitions reshape neighbourhoods, school districts, and regional economies — often concentrating new risks on communities already underserved. I study both the politics of this unevenness and the institutional designs that could change it.

Energy × AI — Infrastructure & Place

AI infrastructure
is urban
infrastructure.

Data centres, cloud systems, and AI pipelines are physical — they consume land, water, and power, and they are governed by policy choices that have real spatial consequences. I examine how US data centre policy shapes cities and communities.

Urban × AI — Future of Work

How AI is
reshaping who
works, and how.

AI is restructuring labour markets and professional life — but its effects aren't neutral. I study how workers navigate precarity, how AI will reshape workforces across industries, and the socio-ethical dimensions of this transformation.

AI & Education

How AI is
redesigning the
classroom.

Generative AI is entering higher education fast — but integration without design is just disruption. I study how institutions can intentionally embed GenAI, how disciplinary perspectives shape adoption, and what this means for learners and educators.
